## Resource policy

This page lists bibliographic references and official publication links only.
No source PDFs are hosted directly on this website.

## Core papers

- **Merkle, R. C., and Hellman, M. E. (1978). _Hiding Information and Signatures in Trapdoor Knapsacks_. IEEE Transactions on Information Theory, 24(5), 525-530.**  
  Why it matters: Original Merkle-Hellman construction and baseline for all later discussion.

- **Shamir, A. (1982). _A Polynomial-time Algorithm for Breaking the Basic Merkle-Hellman Cryptosystem_. Proceedings of FOCS 1982, 145-152.**  
  Why it matters: Landmark result showing the classical scheme can be broken efficiently.

## Cryptanalysis and security perspective

- **Lagarias, J. C., and Odlyzko, A. M. (1985). _Solving Low-Density Subset Sum Problems_. Journal of the ACM, 32(1), 229-246.**  
  Why it matters: Connects subset-sum instances to practical attack feasibility.

- **Brickell, E. F. (1983). _A Survey of Low-Density Knapsack Cryptosystems_. In CRYPTO '82, 3-25.**  
  Why it matters: Early systematic view of security weaknesses in knapsack-based designs.

- **Brickell, E. F. (1984). _Breaking Iterated Knapsack Cryptosystems_. In CRYPTO '83, 342-358.**  
  Why it matters: Demonstrates that modified/iterated variants are also attackable.

- **Odlyzko, A. M. (1990). _The Rise and Fall of Knapsack Cryptosystems_. In Cryptology and Computational Number Theory, 75-88.**  
  Why it matters: Historical and technical synthesis of why knapsack cryptography declined.  
  Link: http://www.dtc.umn.edu/~odlyzko/doc/complete.html

## Variants and alternatives

- **Chor, B., and Rivest, R. L. (1985). _A Knapsack-Type Public Key Cryptosystem Based on Arithmetic in Finite Fields_. In CRYPTO '84, 54-65.**  
  Why it matters: Important non-classical variant used for comparison in the seminar.

## Background references

- **Stanoyevitch, A. (2011). _Discrete Structures with Contemporary Applications_. Chapman & Hall/CRC.**  
  Why it matters: Useful for clean explanations of subset-sum, complexity, and combinatorial foundations.

- **Aumasson, J.-P. (2017). _Serious Cryptography_. No Starch Press.**  
  Why it matters: Modern context for evaluating historical schemes against current cryptographic standards.
